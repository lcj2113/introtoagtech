Construction/OSHA

OSHA 30

As we work through the OSHA-30 module we review the importance of safety and health in the workplace.
OSHA stands for the Occupattional Safety and Health Act. OSHA's mission is to save lives, prevent illness and protect the health of workers.
Created in 1971, by 2007 cut workplace fatalities by 62% and injury/illness by 42%, administers federal safety and health laws to provide safe workplace
Anything in the workplace can be a hazard and it is up to a company, employers and employees to maintain the safety of each other and those in the workplace. For every 3,000 hazards that you encounter in your place of work, you will have 300 near miss experiences. Of those 300 near miss incidents, 30 of them will result in a worker getting injured.
Workers and employees have rights: have a safe workplace, know about hazardous chemicals, see info about injuries in the workplace, training, obtain records, complain without retaliation, and participate in inspections. Employers have the responsibility to enforce this by providing medical personnel, training and training materials, PPE, eliminating or substituting hazards,

Tool Directory

Tools Hammer
Ball Peen Hammer: used with metal, make rivets and indentations
Claw Hammer: has a claw end. Used to drive in and take out nails; a finishing tool
Framing Hammer: roughing tool, used to frame the wooden frame of a house; can also rip nails
Flat Bar: demo tool, that can be used for ripping and lifting
Wrecking Bar: demo tool, to pry
Masonary Hammer: used on stone or rocks
Pick Mattock: used for mining and excavating
Masonary Chisel: used on stone, can be combined with Ball Peen hammer
Wood Chisel: used on wood

Pliers
Long/Needle Nose Pliers: used to grab, bend and cut wires
Wirecutters: cut wires
Linesman: grab, bend, and pull wires, makes holes
Wire stripper: strip wires, has teeth
Tin cutter: cute tin, looks like a garden shear

Wrench
Spud wrench: for steel, fastens nuts and bolts
Combination wrench: can be a open and closed, closed or open
Adjustable wrench: for nuts and bolts
Socket wrench: same as the other wrenches

Screwdrivers
Flathead: flat
Phillips: four way
Robertson: square tip, also four way
Torx: star shaped

Measuring Tool
Torpedo Level: measures vertically or plumb; horizontally or leveled, as well as on a 45 degree angle
Plumbob: lead point tool to measure the midpoint between two parts. Not used as much today
